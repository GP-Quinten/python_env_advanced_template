DEFINITION:
In a clinical study, an outcome measure (often called an endpoint) is the specific variable —or set of variables — that investigators track to determine whether the treatment or exposure being studied makes a difference. In practical terms, it answers: “What exactly will we look at, how will we quantify it, and when, to judge success or harm?”. This is to be distinguished from the medical condition, which defines the population being studied.
