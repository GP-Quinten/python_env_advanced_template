Context:
Patient registries are structured, systematic databases explicitly designed to collect standardized, longitudinal patient-level clinical data from populations defined by specific diseases, conditions, exposures, or treatments. They facilitate observational research, epidemiological monitoring, clinical outcomes evaluation, trend analyses, data-quality assessments, comparative studies, and linkage with other data sources. Registries involve standardized data collection protocols, systematic enrollment, and detailed follow-up.

Administrative healthcare registries created by health systems, hospitals, or governmental healthcare administrations also qualify as registries if they systematically collect patient-level data that can be analyzed for epidemiological research, outcomes studies, or linked with other clinical or administrative datasets.
